Typography Sample

What is this?

This page contains several common tags that you will see on almost any web page. The goal is to style all of the type here so it looks good and reads well reads well.

Get started by looking at the page without any styles. Next apply some styles. Use your judgement and get feedback from anyone available. Listen to what people think and and apply their suggestions.

Challenges

Read the challenges and follow the instructions for each. You will be styling this document along the way. Keep your eye on it and make it look the best you can.

Create a new stylesheet and link it to this document.

Challenge 2 - Define a base font style

Define a font style by setting rules on the body element in your stylesheet.

Set a font-family to a font-stack or a custom font.

Set the font-size, and the line-height to make the font read well. Use your best judegment here.

Challenge 3 - Define heading styles

Define styles for the H1-h6. Do this by setting them to a value in em. For example: h1: 2em. Get the sizes to step down in size. You can also set the weight and color if you think it will look better.

Headings are meant to lead in or label a section. We use them not becuase the text is larger but because we want to say that the text within the h# tag is the name of a section or labels a big idea on the page.

Take a look at the headings on the Bootstrap site. Foundation headings are also worth looking at. Emulate these in your design. Set your styles and keep your on this page. Headings are used throughout, below is a sample of all of the headings h1-h6.

Challenge 4 - Style inline elements

Inline elements are all of the modifcations you to text in a standard paragraph to make it say more.

Inline elements include the strong, em (emphasis), abbr (abbreviation), del (deleted), s (strike-through), and mark (hightlighted). People will use these to increase the communication and nuance of the language written on a page. For example some things are said with a strong voice, other points you will want to emphasize, or mark (imagine highlighting text).

Style the tags below and look at the sample text and other text on this page to see how your styles are working. You can stick with the default style or add your own rules. You can style the font-weight, color, background-color, text-decoration and anything else you can think of.

Challenge 5 - Color

Color is important. By default text is black and the background color of the page is white. This is rather harsh, and doesn't look that great. You can do better!

Style the color and background color on the body tag in your stylesheet. Use colors with good contrast. Check your colors on the contrast checker.

Challenge 6 - Links

Links are important. They need to stand out from the text around them and let visitors know that something is clickable and will lead to more information.

Links by default are blue and have an underline. You can change both of these things for an improvement! Links also have styles that apply to their interactive states: hover, active, and visited. You should style these also.

This paragrah contains links to the vaious sections on this page. If you have gotten this far you have created a stylesheet, then you defined a base font, next it was heading styles, follwed by inline styles, and then colors.

You should style the :hover, :active, and :visited pseudo classes. Read about these here.

Challenge 7 - Blockquotes

Blocks quotes are used present a quoted block of text. Usually you'll also add a citation which says where the quote came from or who it is attributed to.

The blockquote is a tag, the citation is another tag, and you might use more tags in conjunction with these to make a good looking blockquote. Take a look at the markup used by Foundation and Bootstrap. Then write some styles for the blockquote and the cite tag when it appears inside a blockquote.
